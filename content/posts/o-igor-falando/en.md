# Just Igor, Talking

I deleted two long posts that should have come out before this one. Why? It wasn't Igor writing — it was the AI steering me toward a place I never wanted to reach.

I'd gone deep into that plan. The idea was to use AI discreetly across every post. I even built two custom skills for it: one to apply my voice to drafts — trained on work presentations, on pieces I'd written, on research notes — and another to take that output and slot it into the `.md` style guide I publish in here.

But even with the AI restricted to editing and formatting — never touching the text itself — I could feel something slipping away: the details, the feeling, the critique. Ted Chiang wrote in a New Yorker essay that writing is precisely the act of making choices that aren't the default — and AI, by construction, hands you the most likely one. Even knowing it wasn't doing the writing, I had the sense that the writing wasn't Igor's anymore. That made me rethink the whole blog workflow.

I believe anyone who comes here, or who subscribes to the newsletter, wants to hear what I have to say — not what a well-tuned creativity + formatting pipeline produces. So from here on, everything you read is mine, no AI. Just Igor, talking with you.
